A positron emission tomography (PET) scan is a type of imaging test that uses radioactive tracer to look for abnormalities in tissues and organs. The machine detects pairs of gamma rays emitted indirectly by positron-emitting radioactive materials. By monitoring the metabolic processes in the body, a colorful three dimensional image is produced. Many radioactive tracers are used in PET to image selective tissue due to specificity metabolism of each tissue.

PET scan is heavily used in clinical oncology for imaging of tumors, and in neurology for brain activity. PET scan also show problems at the cellular level. Targeting disease such as coronary artery disease, brain tumors, memory disorders, and seizures.
